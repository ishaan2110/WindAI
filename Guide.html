Welcome & The Kaizen Mindset

Welcome, Builder!

You don't need to be a developer to contribute something real today. This guide walks you through every single step of the Build Kaizen process — from idea to a working, integration-ready component. Follow each step in order and you'll have a Wind.AI-ready widget built faster than you think.

🙌

"Anyone Can Build" — Yes, That Means You

The bar to build something real has never been lower. You do not need to know how to code. You need an idea, an AI tool, and this guide. Modern AI tools have fundamentally changed what "building software" means.

🚀

The MVP-First Mindset

Speed over perfection. These mini-apps and widgets can be created very easily. Your job today is not to ship a production-grade app — it's to prove that an idea works. A rough widget that exists today beats a perfect one still in your head next week.

💡
Kaizen Principle

Build the core. Make it work. Ship it for integration. Don't get caught adding every feature or perfecting every detail.

The Full Step-by-Step Pipeline

Work through every phase and step below in order. Each phase has a defined action, a designated tool, and an expected output. Do not skip ahead.

🧩

Phase 0 — Select What to Build

START HERE
STEP 0.1 Choose Your Use Case and Item
ActionBrowse the provided use case and item list. Select the mini-app or widget you will build. If pre-assigned, confirm your assignment.
ToolShared use case / item list (provided by your facilitator)
OutputOne clearly identified item selected — you know its name and what it does.
  • I have selected my mini-app or widget from the list.
  • I know the name of what I am building.
STEP 0.2 Understand the Idea Before You Build It
ActionBefore writing any AI prompt, take a few minutes to think through your selected item. Read any available context, description, or documentation.
ToolUse case documentation, your facilitator, or your own notes
OutputA clear picture of what this app looks like, how it behaves, and what a user would do with it.
🛑
Critical Requirement — Do Not Skip

You must "flesh out how exactly they would look, feel, and what they would do" before starting to generate any code. Ask yourself: What does the screen look like? What can the user interact with? What is the one core thing this widget must do? Spending 2–3 minutes here saves significant rework later.

  • I can describe what my app / widget looks like.
  • I can describe what a user does with it.
  • I can describe the single most important thing it needs to do.
🧠

Phase 1 — Define the Idea with AI

AI CONVERSATION
STEP 1.1 Start Your AI Conversation First
ActionOpen your AI tool and begin a conversation about your idea. This is the very first required action in the build process. Talk through your concept with AI to sharpen and clarify it — do not try to write code yourself.
ToolM365 Copilot, Claude, or AMP ⭐ (recommended — fully verified)
OutputAn open AI conversation session focused on your specific use case.
💬
Think of AI as your co-designer right now

In this step you are having a conversation to define and refine the idea — not to build it yet. The build comes in Phase 2.

  • I have opened my AI tool of choice.
  • I have started a new conversation focused on my selected use case.
STEP 1.2 Ask Structured Questions to Define the App
ActionUse your AI conversation to define: (1) what the app does, (2) what specific features it needs, and (3) what goal it accomplishes. Refine until you have a clear, agreed-upon definition.
ToolM365 Copilot, Claude, or AMP ⭐
OutputA clear written functional definition of your widget — what it does, what it includes, and what it's for.

Suggested conversation starters:

Starter Prompt — Adapt as Needed
"I want to build a mini-app that helps users [describe task].
Can you help me define exactly what this should do
and what features it needs?"
🚨
Crucial Constraint — Mandatory

At some point during this phase, you must explicitly tell the AI:

"This needs to be a single file HTML app."

This is not optional. It is a core technical constraint for the entire pipeline. If you forget this, your output will not be compatible with the conversion process in Phase 4.

  • I have defined what my app does.
  • I have defined the required features.
  • I have defined the goal / outcome for the user.
  • I have explicitly told the AI this must be a single file HTML app.
✅ Phase 1 Output A clear, written functional definition of your widget — what it does, what it contains, and what constraints apply.
🧱

Phase 2 — Generate the Application (HTML)

BUILD IT
STEP 2.1 Ask AI to Generate the Build
ActionOnce your functional definition is locked in from Phase 1, issue the generation prompt to your AI tool. Use the exact prompt language below.
ToolM365 Copilot, Claude, or AMP ⭐
OutputThe AI begins generating a complete HTML application.

📋 Use this exact prompt — copy it word for word:

Exact Generation Prompt
"Build me a single file HTML app that accomplishes this"
  • I have issued the generation prompt to my AI tool.
  • The AI has begun generating code.
STEP 2.2 Receive, Inspect, and Save the HTML File
ActionReview the HTML output. Copy the full HTML content. Open a text editor (Notepad, VS Code, TextEdit, etc.), paste the content, and save with a .html extension. Open in a browser to confirm it works.
ToolAI tool (output) + any text editor + any web browser
OutputA saved .html file on your local machine that opens in a browser and works as a functional prototype.
💡
Tips for This Step

Select all code from <!DOCTYPE html> to </html>. Save with a meaningful name (e.g., task-tracker-widget.html). Double-click to open in a browser and test. If something is broken, ask the AI to fix it: "The submit button doesn't do anything — can you fix that?"

  • I have copied the full HTML output from the AI.
  • I have saved it as a .html file on my computer.
  • I have opened it in a browser and confirmed it functions.
✅ Phase 2 Output A functional prototype — a working .html app file saved on your local machine.
📂

Phase 3 — Prepare Conversion Package

PACKAGE IT
STEP 3.1 Create Your Working Folder with Exactly Two Files
ActionCreate a new folder on your computer. Place exactly two items inside it: your HTML file from Phase 2 and the magic template.md file provided by your facilitator.
ToolYour computer's file system + the provided magic template.md file
OutputA single local folder containing exactly two files — no more, no less.

📁 Your folder must contain EXACTLY these two files:

📁 your-widget-name/
    📄 your-app-name.html    ← your output from Phase 2
    📄 magic template.md    ← provided — do not modify yet
⚠️
Missing the magic template.md?

Stop and ask your floor facilitator before proceeding. This file is required for the conversion in Phase 4.

  • I have created a new local folder with a clear name.
  • My .html file is inside the folder.
  • The magic template.md file is inside the folder.
  • There are only two files in the folder.
STEP 3.2 Upload the Folder to OneDrive
ActionUpload your working folder (containing both files) to your designated OneDrive location for standardized tracking and backup.
ToolOneDrive (web or desktop app)
OutputYour two-file folder is visible and accessible in OneDrive.
  • I have logged into OneDrive.
  • I have uploaded my folder to the designated location.
  • I can see both files are present in OneDrive.
✅ Phase 3 Output A standardized input package — a two-file folder saved locally and backed up to OneDrive — ready for conversion.
⚙️

Phase 4 — Convert to Wind.AI Component

CONVERT IT
STEP 4.1 Open Your AI Tool for Conversion
ActionOpen M365 Copilot, Claude, or AMP for the conversion step. Start a fresh session for this phase.
ToolM365 Copilot, Claude, or AMP ⭐
OutputAn active AI tool session ready to receive your conversion prompt.
💡
Reminder

If M365 Copilot or Quick are unavailable, AMP is your go-to alternative. It is fully verified and working for this exact task.

  • I have opened my AI tool and started a new session.
STEP 4.2 Provide the Conversion Prompt (The "Super Prompt")
ActionTake the "super prompt" conversion template provided by your facilitator and copy-paste it into your AI session, along with the full contents of both your .html file and the magic template.md file.
ToolThe provided super prompt template + your AI tool
OutputThe AI has received all necessary inputs and is ready to run the conversion.

How to submit the conversion prompt:

  1. Open the super prompt template provided by your facilitator.
  2. Copy the entire super prompt.
  3. Paste it into your AI chat.
  4. Include the contents of your .html file (paste the code directly, or attach if your tool supports it).
  5. Include the contents of the magic template.md file in the same way.
  6. Send the message.
  • I have the super prompt template ready.
  • I have included my full HTML file content.
  • I have included the full magic template.md content.
  • I have sent the prompt to the AI.
STEP 4.3 Run the Conversion and Collect the Output
ActionAllow the AI to process the inputs. It will generate a TypeScript component formatted for Wind.AI. Review the output, copy it in full, and save it as a new file.
ToolYour AI tool (output) + text editor
OutputA complete Wind.AI-ready TypeScript component file.
  • The AI has completed the conversion.
  • I have reviewed the TypeScript output.
  • I have copied and saved the TypeScript component to a file.
✅ Phase 4 Output A Wind.AI-ready TypeScript component — the conversion of your HTML prototype into a format the engineering team can directly integrate.
🔌

Phase 5 — Integration

SHIP IT
STEP 5.1 Hand Off to the Development Team
ActionDeliver your TypeScript component file to the engineering / development team designated for integration at this Kaizen. Share the file, briefly explain what the widget does, and confirm receipt.
ToolYour TypeScript component file + floor facilitator or dev team contact
OutputA live, working widget or mini-app running inside the Wind.AI platform.
⚡
Good News

Once developers receive your TypeScript component, they can implement and integrate it within just a few hours. Your work enables theirs — and it moves fast.

  • I have my TypeScript component file ready to share.
  • I have identified the dev team contact or floor facilitator for handoff.
  • I have delivered the file and confirmed receipt.
✅ Phase 5 Output A live, working widget or mini-app running inside Wind.AI — your idea, realized.

Important Event Rules & Constraints

Read this section carefully before you begin building. These are the operating boundaries for today's Kaizen event.

⚙️

Tech Stack Guardrails Are Being Finalized

Final technology stack guardrails and strict building boundaries are currently being finalized with Krishna. These constraints are not yet fully published. If you have questions about whether a specific approach or tool is within bounds, check with your floor facilitator before proceeding. Do not assume — ask.

🚫

MCP Tools Are Excluded from This Kaizen

MCP (Model Context Protocol) tools are explicitly excluded from this event. No conversion templates currently exist for MCP tools, and they are too complex to teach effectively in this format and timeframe. Do not select, attempt to build, or attempt to convert any MCP-based tools today. If your use case seems to involve MCP, flag it with your facilitator immediately and select a different item.

🕐

Integration Timing — Confirm with Your Facilitator

Whether full integration happens during the Kaizen itself or immediately after the event is still an open question. Do not assume your widget will go live today. Your job is to deliver a working TypeScript component. Sync with your floor facilitator to understand the integration timeline for your specific widget and team.
